Neutscape

Neutscape is an interactive visualization for viral antibody landscapes.

An antibody landscape is a comprehensive view of how well an individual can neutralize viruses from the past, present, and future. It is measured by collecting serum from an individual and assaying for neutralization against a historic panel of viruses.

Individual antibody landscapes reflect personal immune histories. Aggregated antibody landscapes reveal population-level differences in antigenic susceptibility which often corelate with age. Therefore, these data can provide valuable clues on what drives us to be susceptible or immune.

The data in this visualization is from Yang et al (eLife, 2022). Each point in the UMAP plot corresponds to one of 777 sera collected from individuals in 2014. You can hover over a point in the UMAP to view the extent to which an individual was able to neutralize different H3N2 strains, measured using a hemagglutinin inhibition (HI) assay. You can click a point to lock the density and phylogenetic tree plots in place. When a UMAP point is clicked, you can also hover over the nodes in the phylogenetic tree to view the exact HI titer measured. To unlock the density and phylogenetic tree plots, re-click the selected point.
